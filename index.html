Should I buy an IPO first day?
+

Buying an IPO on the first day depends on market conditions and your risk appetite. Research before investing.

How do you know if an IPO is good?
+

A good IPO usually has strong financials, growth prospects, and a reputable underwriter. Analyze the company carefully.

How to check IPO start date?
+

IPO start dates are announced on stock exchange websites and financial news portals.

What is issue size?
+

The issue size refers to the total value of shares or bonds offered by a company in an IPO.

How many shares in a lot?
+

The number of shares in a lot is predetermined and varies based on the IPO.

How is the lot size calculated?
+

Lot size is determined by the issuing company and depends on share price and regulations.

Who decides the IPO price band?
+

The IPO price band is decided by the company and its underwriters based on market valuation.

What is IPO GMP?
+

IPO GMP (Grey Market Premium) indicates the premium at which the IPO shares are trading in the unofficial market.

How many lots should I apply for IPO?
+

Apply for as many lots as your budget and risk tolerance allow, while considering the oversubscription probability.
